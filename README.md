# MSc-Experimental-Thesis-on-Network-Analysis
Politics and Mental Health: A Data-Driven Analysis of Toxicity and Moral Values in Public Discourse
## Overview

This project analyzes the structural and semantic dynamics of online public discourse by comparing two classes of information environments:

* **Polluted Information Environments (PIEs)**, characterized by polarization and antagonistic interactions
* **Supportive Information Environments (SIEs)**, characterized by prosocial behavior and mutual support

The study leverages Reddit data (2017–2020), focusing on:

* Political discussions related to the **2016 U.S. presidential election**
* **Mental health communities** in the U.S.

## Research Objectives

The project aims to investigate how different thematic contexts affect:

* **Network topology** and interaction patterns
* **Toxicity propagation** and temporal dynamics
* **Moral framing** in user-generated content

## Methodological Framework

The analysis integrates:

* **Social Network Analysis (SNA)**

  * Construction of user interaction graphs (reply networks)
  * Measurement of structural properties (e.g., modularity, centrality, clustering)

* **Natural Language Processing (NLP)**

  * Toxicity detection (interaction-level and user-level)
  * Moral value extraction based on Moral Foundations Theory

* **Text Analysis**

  * Topic and thematic characterization of discussions

## Key Results

### Polluted Information Environments (Political Debate)

* **High modularity and community fragmentation**, indicating strong ideological segregation
* Sparse inter-community edges, suggesting **echo chamber effects**
* Toxicity exhibits **reactive and reinforcing dynamics**, often escalating within clusters
* Moral discourse is dominated by **loyalty and authority**, frequently mobilized to delegitimize out-groups

### Supportive Information Environments (Mental Health)

* **Lower modularity and higher connectivity**, enabling broader interaction across users
* Network structure supports **information and emotional support diffusion**
* Toxicity is **episodic and predominantly self-directed**, reflecting individual distress rather than conflict
* Moral framing emphasizes **fairness, care, and cohesion**, reinforcing prosocial norms

## Insights

* Thematic context significantly shapes both **mesoscopic structure** (community organization) and **interaction dynamics**
* Polarized environments exhibit **structural segregation coupled with sustained antagonistic exchanges**
* Supportive environments maintain **higher cohesion**, yet still contain substantial negative sentiment
* Results highlight the importance of **context-sensitive moderation strategies** grounded in network and semantic signals
